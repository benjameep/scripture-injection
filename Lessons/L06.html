The Doctrine of Christ: Faith in Jesus Christ

Introduction

The Lord declared that the Book of Mormon contains "the fullness of the gospel of Jesus Christ." (D&C 20:9). Within the Book of Mormon, the Savior’s fundamental principles and ordinances necessary for salvation are sometimes referred to as the doctrine of Christ. Faith in Jesus Christ is the first principle of the gospel. The Book of

9 Which contains a record of a fallen people, and the fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles and to the Jews also;

Mormon helps to cultivate faith in Jesus Christ because "no man can be saved, according to the words of Christ, save they shall have faith in his name" (Moroni 7:38).

38 For no man can be saved, according to the words of Christ, save they shall have faith in his name; wherefore, if these things have ceased, then has faith ceased also; and awful is the state of man, for they are as though there had been no redemption made.

38 For no man can be saved, according to the words of Christ, save they shall have faith in his name; wherefore, if these things have ceased, then has faith ceased also; and awful is the state of man, for they are as though there had been no redemption made.

We live in a day of skepticism, doubt, and disbelief in God. Secularism, moral relativism, and atheism continue to erode people’s faith. The Book of Mormon has come forth in our day to strengthen faith in Jesus Christ. It teaches and testifies that the efficacy and virtue of the entire plan of salvation hinges on the mission, ministry, and message of the Savior.

The prophets of the Book of Mormon taught what faith is and why it is essential to have faith in the Son of God. They confounded the false teachings of their day that eroded faith and plainly taught how to exercise faith in the Savior. Throughout the Book of Mormon, we see the blessings that flow from faith in Jesus Christ including visions, healings, guidance, protection, and forgiveness.

While the philosophies of men and the wisdom of the world clamors for our attention in the latter days, there is only one source for salvation and eternal life. Your faith in the Savior will strengthen you to follow the doctrine of Christ and to press forward as you face life’s challenges. Through faith you can receive the same spiritual blessings enjoyed by the people of the Book of Mormon. Your faith in Jesus Christ will lead you to eternal life.

The Doctrine of Christ: Repentance

Introduction

 The second principle of the doctrine of Christ is repentance. In a revelation to the Prophet Joseph Smith, the Lord declared that the Book of Mormon was brought forth so that the Lamanites and those in the latter days "may believe the gospel and rely upon the merits of Jesus Christ, and be glorified through faith in his name, and that through their repentance they might be saved" (D&C 3:20). Having faith in Jesus Christ and in His atonement naturally leads individuals to repent of their sins, believing they can receive forgiveness. As Samuel the

20 And that the Lamanites might come to the knowledge of their fathers, and that they might know the promises of the Lord, and that they may believe the gospel and rely upon the merits of Jesus Christ, and be glorified through faith in his name, and that through their repentance they might be saved. Amen.

Lamanite taught the Nephites "if ye believe on his name ye will repent of all your sins, that thereby ye may have a remission of them through his merits" (Helaman 14:13).

13 And if ye believe on his name ye will repent of all your sins, that thereby ye may have a remission of them through his merits.

13 And if ye believe on his name ye will repent of all your sins, that thereby ye may have a remission of them through his merits.

The world is in dire need of repentance, yet few people have sufficient faith in the Savior to believe they can change. Some in the Christian world even teach that repentance is not necessary and that "faith alone" can save us or that God will save us in our sins. The Book of Mormon affirms that repentance is necessary for our salvation. It teaches that even the vilest of sinners can repent and be changed through the Atonement of Jesus Christ. From the Book of Mormon we learn the essential components of the repentance process. We learn how to be born again, and to become the children of Christ. We learn how we can one day stand spotless before the judgment seat of Christ.

Regardless of what you may have done in the past, you can repent of your sins and be forgiven. The Lord can loose you from the chains of sin and deliver you from the bondage of addiction. Whether your sins be great or small, you can receive the sweet gift of forgiveness as you fully repent of all your sins.

      21 Yea, I say unto you, my son, that there could be nothing so exquisite and so bitter as were my pains. Yea, and again I say unto you, my son, that on the other hand, there can be nothing so exquisite and sweet as was my joy.

      23 But behold, my limbs did receive their strength again, and I stood upon my feet, and did manifest unto the people that I had been born of God.

      24 Yea, and from that time even until now, I have labored without ceasing, that I might bring souls unto repentance; that I might bring them to taste of the exceeding joy of which I did taste; that they might also be born of God, and be filled with the Holy Ghost.

  3. True repentance leads to confessing, forsaking our sins, and repairing any injuries we may have caused.
    1. The Sons of Mosiah show by their actions that they have been born again (Mosiah 27:34–37).

    2. King of the Anti-Nephi-Lehi recounts how his people were forgiven and encourages his people to bury their swords as a symbol of their complete repentance (Alma 24:6–19).

      3. 6 Now there was not one soul among all the people who had been converted unto the Lord that would take up arms against their brethren; nay, they would not even make any preparations for war; yea, and also their king commanded them that they should not.

      7 Now, these are the words which he said unto the people concerning the matter: I thank my God, my beloved people, that our great God has in goodness sent these our brethren, the Nephites, unto us to preach unto us, and to convince us of the traditions of our wicked fathers.

      11 And now behold, my brethren, since it has been all that we could do (as we were the most lost of all mankind) to repent of all our sins and the many murders which we have committed, and to get God to take them away from our hearts, for it was all we could do to repent sufficiently before God that he would take away our stain—

      14 And the great God has had mercy on us, and made these things known unto us that we might not perish; yea, and he has made these things known unto us beforehand, because he loveth our souls as well as he loveth our children; therefore, in his mercy he doth visit us by his angels, that the plan of salvation might be made known unto us as well as unto future generations.

      15 Oh, how merciful is our God!       16 And now, my brethren, if our brethren seek to destroy us, behold, we will hide away our swords, yea, even we will bury them deep in the earth, that they may be kept bright, as a testimony that we have never used them, at the last day; and if our brethren destroy us, behold, we shall go to our God and shall be saved.
