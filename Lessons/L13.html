Becoming a Zion People

A Pattern for Preparing for the Second Coming

Introduction

The Book of Mormon was uniquely written to help us in our day to prepare for the Second Coming of the Lord Jesus Christ. President Ezra Taft Benson (1899–1994) taught the following:

"In the Book of Mormon we find a pattern for preparing for the Second Coming. A major portion of the book centers on the few decades just prior to Christ’s coming to America. By careful study of that time period, we can determine why some were destroyed in the terrible judgments that preceded His coming and what brought others to stand at the temple in the land of Bountiful and thrust their hands into the wounds of His hands and feet" (Teachings of presidents of the Church—Ezra Taft Benson, p.140).

The disciples of Christ in the Book of Mormon resisted the evils of their day and remained faithful as they waited for the fulfillment of the prophesies of the coming of the Savior.

As we follow the guidance of prophets in our day, we will be prepared to watch for the signs of the Savior's Second Coming and be prepared to meet Him.

The Attributes of Zion

Introduction

The Book of Mormon teaches the principles and patterns for establishing Zion. The Prophet Joseph Smith taught that "The building up of Zion is a cause that has interested the people of God in every age; it is a theme upon which prophets, priests and kings have dwelt with peculiar delight; they have looked forward with joyful anticipation to the day in which we live; and fired with heavenly and joyful anticipations they have sung and written and prophesied of this our day; but they died without the sight; we are the favored people that God has made choice of to bring about the Latter-day glory" (Teachings of Presidents of the Church: Joseph Smith, 186).

The people of God have always sought to establish Zion, a people of righteousness, unity, and love. The prophets of the Book of Mormon repeatedly taught their people to be obedient, to love others, and to care for the poor and needy. Throughout their history, the Nephites experienced times of peace, unity, and righteousness, but each of these episodes were eventually followed by pride, contention, wickedness, and war.

As we prepare for the Second Coming of Jesus Christ, the Book of Mormon teaches us how we can establish Zion despite the wickedness of the world around us. Like the righteous Nephites, we can remain steadfast and immovable in keeping the commandments, caring for the poor and needy, and being filled with love towards God and towards all men.
