The Doctrine of Christ: Baptism, the Sacrament, and the Gift of the Holy Ghost

The Doctrine of Christ: Baptism & the Sacrament

Introduction

The Book of Mormon teaches the "fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ," (D&C 20:9) and was written to restore "plain and precious truths which have been taken away from [the bible]" (1 Nephi 13:40). Baptism is the first ordinance of the gospel of Jesus Christ, and the Book of Mormon restores plain and precious truths regarding baptism and the sacrament.

40 And the angel spake unto me, saying: These last records, which thou hast seen among the Gentiles, shall establish the truth of the first, which are of the twelve apostles of the Lamb, and shall make known the plain and precious things which have been taken away from them; and shall make known to all kindreds, tongues, and people, that the Lamb of God is the Son of the Eternal Father, and the Savior of the world; and that all men must come unto him, or they cannot be saved.

9 Which contains a record of a fallen people, and the fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles and to the Jews also;

During the Apostasy, sacred truths regarding the ordinances of the gospel of Jesus Christ were lost. Fundamental questions over how to baptize, who could be baptized, and whether baptism was essential for salvation were debated and argued. The meaning and purpose of the ordinance of the sacrament and other doctrinal differences led to schisms, fractures, and splinter groups in the Christian world. The restoration of the church began with the coming forth of the Book of Mormon which answers many questions pertaining to these ordinances of the gospel in plainness and simplicity. It affirms that baptism is a saving ordinance which has been required as part of the gospel of Jesus Christ from the beginning.

During the Savior’s personal ministry among the Nephites, he announced the fulfillment of the Law of Moses and instituted baptism and the sacrament as two of the essential ordinances of the higher law. Ordinances unlock the power of the Atonement of Jesus Christ in our lives. As you strive to keep the covenants that you have made in the waters of baptism and in the sacrament each week, you can be forgiven of your sins and receive the blessings of the Savior’s Atonement.

  4. The Book of Mormon teaches that little children are alive in Christ and do not need baptism until they are accountable before God.
    1. Mormon teaches his son Moroni why the baptism of little children is a false practice (Moroni 8:5–26).

      2. 5 For, if I have learned the truth, there have been disputations among you concerning the baptism of your little children.

      6 And now, my son, I desire that ye should labor diligently, that this gross error should be removed from among you; for, for this intent I have written this epistle.

      7 For immediately after I had learned these things of you I inquired of the Lord concerning the matter. And the word of the Lord came to me by the power of the Holy Ghost, saying:

      8 Listen to the words of Christ, your Redeemer, your Lord and your God. Behold, I came into the world not to call the righteous but sinners to repentance; the whole need no physician, but they that are sick; wherefore, little children are whole, for they are not capable of committing sin; wherefore the curse of Adam is taken from them in me, that it hath no power over them; and the law of circumcision is done away in me.

      9 And after this manner did the Holy Ghost manifest the word of God unto me; wherefore, my beloved son, I know that it is solemn mockery before God, that ye should baptize little children.

      10 Behold I say unto you that this thing shall ye teach—repentance and baptism unto those who are accountable and capable of committing sin; yea, teach parents that they must repent and be baptized, and humble themselves as their little children, and they shall all be saved with their little children.

      11 And their little children need no repentance, neither baptism. Behold, baptism is unto repentance to the fulfilling the commandments unto the remission of sins.

      12 But little children are alive in Christ, even from the foundation of the world; if not so, God is a partial God, and also a changeable God, and a respecter to persons; for how many little children have died without baptism!

      13 Wherefore, if little children could not be saved without baptism, these must have gone to an endless hell.

      14 Behold I say unto you, that he that supposeth that little children need baptism is in the gall of bitterness and in the bonds of iniquity; for he hath neither faith, hope, nor charity; wherefore, should he be cut off while in the thought, he must go down to hell.

      15 For awful is the wickedness to suppose that God saveth one child because of baptism, and the other must perish because he hath no baptism.

      16 Wo be unto them that shall pervert the ways of the Lord after this manner, for they shall perish except they repent. Behold, I speak with boldness, having authority from God; and I fear not what man can do; for perfect love casteth out all fear.

      17 And I am filled with charity, which is everlasting love; wherefore, all children are alike unto me; wherefore, I love little children with a perfect love; and they are all alike and partakers of salvation.

      18 For I know that God is not a partial God, neither a changeable being; but he is unchangeable from all eternity to all eternity.

      19 Little children cannot repent; wherefore, it is awful wickedness to deny the pure mercies of God unto them, for they are all alive in him because of his mercy.

For the power of redemption cometh on all them that have no law; wherefore, he that is not condemned, or he that is under no condemnation, cannot repent; and unto such baptism availeth nothing—

  5. We must be sufficiently worthily to partake of the sacrament.
    1. Jesus teaches the disciples not to let anyone partake of the sacrament unworthily (3 Nephi 18:28–32).

The Gift of the Holy Ghost and Enduring to the End

Introduction

As part of the "fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ," (D&C 20:9) the Book of Mormon teaches that we must receive the gift of the Holy Ghost and endure to the end in order to be saved. As Nephi testified "unless a man shall endure to the end, in following the example of the Son of God, he cannot be saved" (2 Nephi 31:16).   

16 And now, my beloved brethren, I know by this that unless a man shall endure to the end, in following the example of the Son of the living God, he cannot be saved.

9 Which contains a record of a fallen people, and the fulness of the gospel of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles and to the Jews also;

In our world today many people feel discouraged, lost, or hopeless. They wonder whether they can have a happy marriage, be a good parent, or handle the relentless temptations of the world. The Savior has provided us with all the spiritual blessings and gifts we need in order to overcome the world and its challenges. He has provided us with the gift of the Holy Ghost, or the blessing of being influenced, guided, and blessed by a member of the Godhead.

Some wonder why they really need the gift of the Holy Ghost. The Book of Mormon teaches that it is through the Holy Ghost that we can be baptized with fire and receive a remission of our sins. This sanctifying gift is essential to standing spotless before God at the last day. Through the gift of the Holy Ghost we can also receive spiritual gifts, including receiving guidance and direction in our lives. The gift of the Holy Ghost helps us to endure to the end.

As you prayerfully seek the guidance and companionship of the Holy Ghost and remain worthy of its influence, "it will show unto you all things what ye should do" (2 Nephi 32:5). The comfort, peace, faith, and strength you can receive from the Spirit can help you to overcome all of the trials of your life. As you keep your covenants, you can always have the Spirit with you to teach you how to follow Jesus Christ and obtain eternal life.

5 For behold, again I say unto you that if ye will enter in by the way, and receive the Holy Ghost, it will show unto you all things what ye should do.

  1. The Book of Mormon teaches that the Holy Ghost is a member of the Godhead and a witness of the Father and the Son.
    1. In Nephi’s discourse on the doctrine of Christ, he teaches that the Holy Ghost is a witness of the Father and  the Son (2 Nephi 31:18, 21).

And now, behold, this is the doctrine of Christ, and the only and true doctrine of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost, which is one God, without end. Amen.
